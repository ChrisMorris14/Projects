⚾ Owen's 9 Innings
  1. Show up every day — Especially when you don't feel like it. Difference between good and GREAT.
  2. 60 minutes, no excuses — Aaron Judge, Bryce Harper, and Mike Trout put in the time. So do you.
  3. Master the boring — No one wants to do it. If you do, you get ahead.
  4. Eat / Hydrate / Rest is training — Eat for performance. Bad foods slow you down. Muscles grow while you sleep.
  5. Form beats reps — A perfect push-up beats sloppy form.
  6. Track your reps — Beat last week's you — that's the only competition that matters right now.
  7. Core and rotation — That's where bat speed is made.
  8. Protect the shoulders — Band external rotation and face pulls are never optional.
  9. Have fun — The athletes who love the work become the athletes everyone else tries to catch up to.

⌛ 60-Min Blueprint
7m Warm-UpGet blood moving. Raise body temp. Prime joints before any load. Jump rope, high knees, arm circles, leg swings. 0:00–0:07
5m ActivationWake up the muscles you’re about to use. Glute bridges, band pull-aparts, bird dogs, cat-cow. Tells the body what’s coming. 0:07–0:12
18m Main Work AHardest movements first when you’re freshest. 2–4 sets. Primary lifts, big compound exercises, or heavy skill work for the day. 0:12–0:30
16m Main Work BSecondary and accessory work. Supports Main A. Builds the supporting muscles that protect joints and fill in weak spots. 0:30–0:46
8m 🔥 FinisherPush hard for 8 minutes. This is where conditioning is built. Ph1: 20s on/20s off ×3 · Ph2: 30s on/15s off ×3 · Ph3+4: TABATA 20s/10s ×8 rounds. 0:46–0:54
6m Cool-DownStatic stretches and deep breathing. Lowers heart rate, reduces soreness, protects flexibility. Never skip — this is when your body starts recovering. 0:54–1:00
